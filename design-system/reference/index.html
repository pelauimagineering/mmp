Four design principles.

Felt up close, plaque from across the room. Both products earn their voice from the same four rules.

01

Bound by brotherhood

Every screen earns familiarity. First names. Real banter. Never “User 4.”

02

One trophy, two tables

Poker and Golf share tokens, voice, plaque chrome — but each owns its own theme.

03

One-handed, in the moment

Designed for a phone in one hand, drink in the other. 44px hit-targets, slide-to-confirm.

04

Plaque-grade typography

Fraunces in display sets the trophy tone. Inter handles the work. Mono for the score.

Voice, at a glance.

Talking about the league should sound like the guys at the table, not a fitness app.

We say

✓“Tony, where’s your buy-in?”
✓“Round 1 tees off in 1h 42m.”
✓“Harish, you’re back-to-back. Plaque-worthy.”
✓“Slide to fold.”

We don’t say

✕“Player 4 has been notified.”
✕“Tournament event T-1h 42m.”
✕“Congratulations on your achievement!”
✕“Click here to forfeit hand.”
